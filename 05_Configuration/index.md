# Section 5 — Conceptual Design & Configuration Selection

**PART II — MISSION ANALYSIS & CONCEPTUAL DESIGN**

> *What does the vehicle look like, at the broadest level, and why?*

Configuration selection is where design intuition meets engineering constraint. The choice of wing
position, tail layout, and fuselage architecture made at this stage determines what every subsequent
discipline inherits: the aerodynamicist's drag budget, the structural engineer's load paths, and
the pilot's handling characteristics. This section documents every major fixed-wing and rotorcraft
configuration with full-scale and RC analysis separately.
